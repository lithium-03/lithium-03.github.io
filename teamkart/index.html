About Formula Student

Formula Student (FS) is Europe's most established educational motorsport competition, run by the Institution of Mechanical Engineers. Backed by the industry and high profile engineers such as their Patron Ross Brawn OBE, the competition aims to inspire and develop enterprising and innovative young engineers. Universities from across the globe are challenged to design and build a single-seat racing car in order to compete in static and dynamic events, which demonstrate their understanding and test the performance of the vehicle.

It seeks to challenge university students to conceive, design, build, cost, present and compete as a team with a small single-seat racing car in a series of static and dynamic competitions. The format of the event is such that it provides an ideal opportunity for the students to demonstrate and improve their capabilities to deliver a complex and integrated product in the demanding environment of a motorsport competition.

It is held across five continents and more than 300 universities participate in it. The cars are judges in three different categories:

  • static inspection of engineering design
  • solo performance trials
  • high performance track endurance

Formula Student aims to take students out of the classroom and give them a chance to gain real hands-on experience.

Our Cars

We have completed the design and manufacture of 3 cars over the past few years.

Our first car, the KX-1, was completed in 18 months. We participated in Formula Student 2010 thus making us the only team from Eastern India and amongst the few Indian teams to be a part of this prestigious event. We stood 9th in the cost report analysis, making us one of the most cost-efficient teams We were amongst the 6 shortlisted teams for the Airbus Team Work Award.

Having completed our second car, the K-1, in just 7 months, we participated in Formula Student 2011. This being the first time we actually took the car to the event, we managed to outperform the other rookie teams. We are amongst the top 3 rookie teams at the event.

Just like the previous year, we were once again nominated for the Airbus Team Work Award.

We started designing our our third car, the K-2, in the December of 2011. The manufacture of the car took us about 5 months and we began its testing in the June of 2012. Having finished and optimizing our vehicle we are now waiting for the onset of FSAE-2013 with bated breath! We hope to not just match but to improve upon our previous performances at the event.

Our Team

Formula SAE at IIT Kharagpur took a solid form in October 2008, when a group of enthusiastic students got together to form TeamKART. It was started up as a project under the aegis of the Sponsored Research and Industrial Consultancy Cell (SRIC) of IIT Kharagpur and under the purview of the Mechanical Department. Since then there has been a steady rise in our workforce and our potential as a team.

Our journey began with our first car, the KX-1. We faced many problems in our fledgling years, mainly due to a lack of financial aid and technical knowledge. However with the help of some determination and a lot of perseverance, we rose above all obstacles and managed to build our car. Our first successful run gave a huge boost to our confidence and also served the purpose of showing the Institute, our sponsors and our alumni, our ambition and determination to succeed. After that we, in the span of less than 2 years, achieved the feat of competing in an international event with our second car, the K-1.

We are guided by our extremely competent faculty from the Department of Mechanical Engineering. Our faculty advisors are: Prof. Amiya R. Mohanty Prof. Kingshook Bhattacharyya Prof C.S. Kumar

We have completed the manufacture of our third car and are awaiting our participation in FSAE-2013.

Become A Sponsor

TeamKART is always on the lookout for new partners who can help the project. With the advent of F1 the popularity of formula racing in India is treading an exponential curve, making the media take note of TeamKART more and more. We always try to highlight our relationship with the sponsors and express our gratitude via the same media.

But its not only our public outreach that we bring to the table, sponsoring us also provides access to highly motivated and skilled potential employees.
